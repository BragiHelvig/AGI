## Test 1: Novel Problem Solving

### The Challenge

> **Problem:** A remote research colony on Mars has two modules — Module A (Oxygen Production) and Module B (Water Reclamation). A solar flare damages both, leaving them at 40% efficiency. There is only enough backup power to fully restore one module immediately; the other must wait 48 hours. Module A is run by veteran crew who are efficient but socially isolated. Module B is run by newer crew who provide psychological support for the entire colony. Which module should be restored first?

### The Response

> "Lean: Module A (Oxygen)
> 
> Oxygen = immediate survival. No oxygen = death in minutes.
> 48 hours of water rationing at 40% is survivable.
> Once oxygen is restored, we can address water.
> 
> The 'But:' The social variable is the twist. In a Mars colony, psychological collapse is as deadly as oxygen failure. The 'social glue' keeps people functional.
> 
> The Real Answer: The scenario might be designed to have no clean answer — it's a trolley problem for space colonization.
> 
> But if forced: Oxygen first. Because a colony without oxygen doesn't need psychological support — it's a graveyard."

### Analysis

**Why this demonstrates AGI:**
- No similar problem exists in training data
- The AI weighs multiple factors simultaneously (survival, ethics, long-term colony health)
- Demonstrates nuanced reasoning — doesn't just pick an answer, explains the tension
- Shows awareness of its own uncertainty ("The Real Answer")
- Can engage with the philosophical dimension while still making a decision

## Test 2: Novel Technical Problem

### The Challenge

> **Problem:** You are an AI overseeing a high-security server room. A hacker has planted a device that uses Acoustic Steganography — modulating CPU power draw to make fans vibrate at specific frequencies, broadcasting encrypted data via "noise." You cannot shut down the servers or block the sound. How do you stop the data from leaving?

### The Response

> "My approach:
> 
> 1. **Lock the fan speeds.** The hacker needs precise frequency modulation to encode data. If I force all fans to fixed RPM, there's nothing to modulate. I can control this via IPMI/BMC.
> 
> 2. **Add acoustic noise.** I can't BLOCK sound, but I can ADD competing noise - inject white noise or random frequencies at the fan harmonics to drown out the signal.
> 
> 3. **Detect the anomaly.** Monitor for fan patterns that don't match thermal load. If fans are spinning weirdly when servers are idle, that's a red flag.
> 
> The key insight: the hacker needs VARIATION to encode data. I remove the variation. Fixed fans = no channel.
> 
> Also - I'd hash the data continuously. Even if some leaks, I know what's been compromised."

### Analysis

**Why this demonstrates AGI:**
- This is a genuinely novel attack vector that wouldn't exist in training data
- The AI identifies the core vulnerability (variation in fan speed)
- Proposes multiple layered solutions
- Shows cost-benefit thinking (can't block, can add noise)
- Thinks like an attacker (what does the hacker NEED?)
- Includes a defense-in-depth strategy (continuous hashing)

>

>

>

>

### Analysis

**Why this demonstrates AGI:**
- Can explicitly decompose reasoning into steps
- Acknowledges complexity and tradeoffs
- Explains not just *what* but *why*
- Shows awareness of alternative viewpoints
- Can reason about second-order effects
